Bogland Projects in Ireland

1. The Living Bog Project (2016–2021):

This was Ireland's largest raised bog restoration initiative, targeting 12 raised bog Special Areas of Conservation (SACs) across seven counties. The project restored over 2,600 hectares of raised bog habitat, aiming to increase the national active raised bog area by 50%. It also focused on community engagement and education to promote peatland conservation. RAISED BOGS

2. Bord na Móna Peatland Restoration:

Bord na Móna, Ireland's semi-state company responsible for peat extraction, has shifted towards peatland rehabilitation. The company is on track to restore over 8,125 hectares of bog to peat-forming conditions, with more than 7,200 hectares restored to date. This effort contributes to carbon sequestration and biodiversity enhancement. BORD NA MÓNA

3. Coillte's Peatland Habitat Restoration:

Coillte, Ireland's state forestry company, has been involved in EU LIFE-funded restoration projects over the past decade. Their efforts focus on improving the quality of specific habitats on their estate, including boglands and forests, contributing to national conservation goals. COILLTE

4. Girley Bog Restoration:

Led by the Irish Peatland Conservation Council (IPCC), this community-driven project focuses on conserving Girley Bog in County Meath. Activities include volunteering, education, and awareness events to promote peatland conservation at the local level. IPCC These projects reflect Ireland's commitment to restoring peatlands, recognizing their importance in climate regulation, biodiversity conservation, and cultural heritage.
