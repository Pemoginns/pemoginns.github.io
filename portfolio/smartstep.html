Scope of the Project

The project aims to design a user-friendly, interactive mobile application focused on fitness and health tracking, incorporating key features such as balance tracking, calorie counting, and an interactive map. The goal is to ensure the app is both functional and engaging, with an emphasis on human-computer interaction (HCI) principles to create a seamless experience.

Key Elements of the Scope

  • Target Audience: Fitness and health enthusiasts, including athletes and casual users tracking diet/workouts.
  • Core Features:
    • Balance Tracker: Monitoring posture or body balance during workouts.
    • Calorie Counter: Tracks calories consumed vs. calories burned.
    • Interactive Map: Tracks workout routes with GPS functionality.
  • Design Phases:
    • Lo-Fi Prototyping: Basic functionality with simple designs.
    • Lo-Fi Design II: Further refined design with added color and planning for additional pages.
    • Hi-Fi Prototype: Interactive prototype created with Uizard.
  • User Experience (UX) and Interface Design: Focus on making the app intuitive, familiar, and engaging, with inspiration from popular apps like fitness trackers, Deliveroo Rider, and Twitter.

Deliverables

  • Low-fidelity prototypes (Lo-Fi I & II)
  • High-fidelity interactive prototype (Hi-Fi) created with Uizard
  • Documentation explaining design rationale and choices

Timeline and Milestones

  • Lo-Fi Phase: Conceptualization and basic design ideas.
  • Lo-Fi II Phase: Refinement of design elements.
  • Hi-Fi Phase: Interactive prototype for testing and feedback.
